1 - Lambada


Lambada is a musical genre that originated in Pará in the 1970s but gained greater success in the late 1980s. At the time, its daring music and dance moves with spins and acrobatics caught the attention of the world. Lambada has its roots in African and Latin American rhythms, such as salsa, merengue, and carimbó. These musical styles were brought by African slaves who were taken to Latin America during the colonial period. Over time, these rhythms blended with other genres like pop and rock, creating a unique and contagious sound.
