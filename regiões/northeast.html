1 - Forró


Forró is a popular Brazilian musical genre rooted in northeastern folk music, featuring main rhythms like baião, xote, and arrasta-pé. Baião is marked by syncopated time signatures and the accordion, xote has a softer, danceable rhythm, and arrasta-pé is faster and lively, ideal for parties and traditional dances. Over time, forró has evolved, incorporating influences from rock, jazz, and electronic music, resulting in subgenres like forró universitário and forró eletrônico. Despite these modern variations, traditional forró remains a cherished and celebrated part of Brazilian culture.



2 - Axé


Axé is a vibrant and energetic musical genre that originated in the state of Bahia, Brazil, in the 1980s. Combining elements of Afro-Caribbean, reggae, and Brazilian popular music, Axé is characterized by its infectious rhythms, lively beats, and exuberant performances. The genre is deeply intertwined with the cultural and religious traditions of Bahia, particularly those of Afro-Brazilian heritage.



3 - Frevo


Frevo is a musical genre and dance typical of Pernambuco, known for its fast pace and rhythmic complexity, usually performed by a brass orchestra (trombones, trumpets, clarinets), as well as percussion instruments. The arrangements are marked by the virtuosity of the musicians. It originated in the multicultural context of Recife in the late 19th century, combining elements of European marches with local rhythms such as maxixe and polka. The frevo dance is characterized by acrobatic movements and the use of colorful umbrellas.



4 - Samba de Roda


Cultural manifestation that mixes dance, music and poetry; samba de roda was born in Recôncavo Baiano and expanded throughout the country. The musical genre is a great reference and was awarded the title of Intangible Heritage of Humanity in 2008. With African roots and elements of Portuguese culture, the style is composed of musicians who play instruments such as ganzás, atabaques, reco-reco and viola. Samba de roda gave rise to Bossa Nova and Rio samba.
