1 - Moda de Viola


The "Moda de Viola," a traditional genre from Brazil's Midwest region, revolves around the "viola caipira," a ten-string guitar renowned for its distinctive sound and rich melodies. Its lyrics often delve into rural life, nature, love, and nostalgia, narrating everyday stories from the countryside. This musical style is celebrated through festivals and gatherings that highlight local and regional musicians, reflecting its deep cultural roots and evolving adaptations to contemporary influences. 3.5



2 - Sertanejo Universitário


In the Midwest of Brazil, Sertanejo Universitário is a popular subgenre of Brazilian country music influenced by pop, rock, and electronic elements. It appeals primarily to urban youth and college students with lyrics focusing on themes such as romance, urban life, and youth culture. It uses traditional instruments like acoustic guitar and viola caipira, alongside electric guitar, keyboard, and electronic drums to create modern and dynamic arrangements. Sertanejo universitário is known for its commercial and accessible approach, reflecting its broad appeal and modernization within Brazilian music.
