1 - Fandango


Fandango is a set of popular dances, known as marcas, integrated into Brazilian folklore, especially in the southern part of the country. It originated with the Azorean Portuguese around 1750 and was part of the Intrudo celebrations, the precursor to Carnival. Over time, it was assimilated by the caboclo of the Paraná coast, preserving its folkloric character. Its dances are accompanied by sung poetry and viola, being a vibrant cultural expression rooted in Brazilian identity.



2 - Vanerão


Vanerão is a lively and fast-paced musical and dance style originating from Rio Grande do Sul, a variation of the vanera. This genre is very popular in traditional gaucho balls and festivities. It is characterized by energetic rhythms and broad, leaping steps, reflecting the vivacity and joy of local celebrations. Vanerão is an important cultural expression in preserving the gaucho identity, showcasing the dynamic and spirited nature of the region's folklore.
